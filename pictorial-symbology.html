Beyond Symbology: Identifying Subtextual Themes in Pictorial Maps

  • Independently initiated and conducted a comprehensive analysis of pictorial maps depicting the United States from the 1920-1960s. Applied Cultural and Human Geography and Political Ecology theories to reveal subtextual narratives involving cultural caricaturization, capitalism and consumerism, American nationalism, enclosure and dispossession of land and exploitation of resources, and segregation in the depiction of labor and leisure activities.
  • Explored and analyzed pictorial maps, delving into their symbology and connections to political, economic, cultural, and social dynamics.
  • Presented at the Annual Meeting of the East Lakes Division of the American Association of Geographers (November 2023).
